The Problem

Recycling fails because cities depend on perfect human behavior.

In real life, people are busy. They do not always stop to carefully separate plastic, cans, food waste, and trash. That creates contaminated recycling streams, more landfill waste, and more emissions from inefficient processing.

Too Much Friction

Sustainable behavior often requires extra effort. If recycling feels inconvenient, many people skip it.

Contamination

When food waste or non-recyclables mix with recyclables, entire batches can become harder or more expensive to process.

City-Scale Waste

In dense cities like NYC, even a small improvement in sorting accuracy can create a large environmental benefit at scale.

The Solution

ReSort NYC makes recycling automatic at the infrastructure level.

Instead of expecting every resident to sort perfectly, ReSort uses physical separation, computer vision, robotics, and human quality control to process mixed waste more intelligently.

“ReSort NYC turns recycling from a behavior problem into an infrastructure problem.”

What it is

A smart sorting station for apartment buildings, schools, campuses, sanitation transfer points, and neighborhood waste hubs.

  • Accepts mixed waste input
  • Separates high-value recyclables automatically
  • Flags uncertain items for human review
  • Collects data to improve over time

Why it stands out

Most sustainability ideas depend on changing habits. This one changes the system itself.

  • Reduces dependence on manual sorting
  • Works with real city waste behavior
  • Combines feasible hardware with scalable software
  • Can start small and expand citywide
How It Works

A realistic multi-stage sorting pipeline

The system is designed to sound like something a startup could actually prototype and pilot. Not magic. Not fantasy. A credible automation workflow.

01

Mixed waste enters on a conveyor

Users throw waste into one intake stream. The system moves items across a conveyor for staged analysis and sorting.

02

Magnetic separator removes ferrous metal

Steel cans and other magnetic metals are removed first using a simple, robust physical process.

03

AI vision system identifies remaining materials

A camera system scans each item and classifies likely material types such as plastic bottle, aluminum can, paper carton, food waste, or contamination. This is where the “AI” actually matters.

04

Robotic arm picks high-confidence recyclables

A robotic pick-and-place arm powered by LimeLight removes target items like plastic containers and aluminum cans from the stream and drops them into the correct bins.

05

Human QA handles low-confidence edge cases

Items the model is not confident about go to a final review lane. That makes the system realistic, safer, and easier to defend in front of judges.

06

Data dashboard improves operations over time

The system logs contamination rates, sorting accuracy, material volume, and high-mistake item types, helping cities improve both operations and public education.

Technology

Code, engineering, and automation all have a clear role

Computer Vision Model

Trained to detect and classify visible waste items by material and object type.

Camera + Inference System

Uses a vision device like Limelight or another embedded camera pipeline for fast classification.

Robotic Pick Arm

Executes simple pick-and-place actions for the most valuable recyclable targets.

Conveyor + Sensors

Controls item flow and gives the vision system stable opportunities to inspect objects.

Sorting Logic

Confidence thresholds decide whether an item is auto-sorted or sent to human QA.

Analytics Dashboard

Tracks performance, contamination, trends, and system improvement over time.

Environmental Impact
